Level 7: The Master Ascent

Mechanics Utilized: All learned mechanics, combined into two very distinct, yet still extremely forgiving paths, leading to a common goal.
Design Elements:
Initial Familiar Section: Starts with an exceptionally gentle sequence using single jumps and one extremely slow moving platform.
Major Divergence - Two Fundamentally Different Paths:
Path A (Flow & Timing Challenge / Ultimate Reward): Focuses on continuous movement and subtle timing. Longer sequence of slightly less wide platforms (like Level 4), several barely faster moving platforms (like Level 6), and a short vertical ascent requiring two easy double jumps.
Reward: Reaching the end of this path grants a "Flow State Activation" (a temporary effect that very subtly increases player speed and jump height by 2% for the next 30 seconds, providing a powerful, but still gentle, gameplay boost for potential subsequent levels or timed challenges) AND immediately provides a full health refill.
Path B (Exploration & Minimal Interaction / Assistive Rewards): Longer and less about precision, more about finding the way. Navigating a very simple, wide vertical maze, with a hidden, very short detour behind a simple, pushable block (new interaction) that leads to bonus coins. Platforming mostly on wide stationary platforms or gentle ramps.
Reward: This path is dotted with "Safe Passage Markers" that, when passed, temporarily outline the edges of all upcoming platforms in a bright, reassuring glow. It also has three clearly marked extra checkpoints, and a "secret" area with additional coins and a "Slow Motion Bubble" (a consumable item that, when activated, briefly slows down all moving platforms and environmental elements by 50% for 5 seconds).
Generous Design: Both paths are designed with maximum landing zones and extremely forgiving timings.
No Hazards: No sudden deaths or enemies.
Climax: Both paths ultimately converge on a final, large platform with the level exit.
Challenges:
Applying a mix of learned mechanics based on chosen path, with very little pressure.
Slightly longer sequences of actions compared to Level 6.
Deciding which type of gameplay experience (flow/timing for temporary stat boost vs. exploration/safety for powerful assistive tools) they prefer.
Player Learning:
Consolidating understanding of all core mechanics.
Understanding the concept of choice in paths and how it can fundamentally affect gameplay experience and rewards (temporary stat boosts vs. powerful assistive items).
